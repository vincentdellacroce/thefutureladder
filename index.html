How It Works
Your Blueprint, Year by Year
9th Grade
Explore. Pick the broad field you're drawn to. Start with intro APs. Join one club related to your interest and actually show up.
10th Grade
Narrow down. If engineering looks right, start building. First project, first competition. Take AP Calc AB or AP Physics if you're ready.
11th Grade
Execute. This is the year colleges weight the most. Hardest APs. Lead something. Land an internship or research role. Take the SAT/ACT.
12th Grade
Apply with a story. Every element of your application should point toward one coherent picture of who you are and where you're going.
The rule: Colleges don't admit statistics — they admit narratives. Everything on this site helps you build a coherent, compelling narrative around your chosen field. Pick your major, then work backward.
