🎭 Arts & Media

"Make things that matter. Tell stories that last."
What are the Arts & Media?
Create. Communicate. Shape Culture.

Arts and media encompasses Architecture, Graphic Design, Film & Television, Music, Fine Arts, Communications, Journalism, Photography, Game Design, and related fields. These disciplines train visual thinking, storytelling, craft, and creative problem-solving — skills that are increasingly valuable in a world flooded with content and starving for quality.

Arts careers have real, sustainable paths — but they require intentionality. The difference between a graphic designer at a startup earning $85K and a freelancer struggling at $22K is almost entirely portfolio quality and business sense. Plan accordingly.

The honest reality: Arts fields have a wider income distribution than any other category — from struggling to thriving. The top tier of architects, creative directors, film directors, and music industry executives earn extraordinary incomes. The key differentiator is almost always: the quality of your portfolio + business literacy + relentless networking.

High School Roadmap

  • 9th: Start building. Draw, photograph, design, write, compose. Every day. Volume matters early. Take every art class available.
  • 10th: Identify your specific medium. Start building a portfolio. AP Art History if available. Learn Adobe Creative Suite basics.
  • 11th: AP Studio Art or AP Music Theory. Enter competitions. Seek a mentor. Do a project for a real client — even free. Build your first real portfolio.
  • 12th: Submit to competitions, publications, festivals. Apply to conservatories/art schools with a strong portfolio. Consider dual-track schools (art + business). Your portfolio matters more than your GPA for art school.

What Arts Schools Look For

  • Portfolio — The most important element, full stop. 15–20 pieces showing range and depth.
  • Artist statement — Why you make what you make. Clarity of vision matters.
  • Process work — Sketches, drafts, iterations. Schools want to see how you think, not just the final product.
  • GPA and SAT — Matter less than portfolio but still matter at competitive programs (RISD, Parsons, CalArts)
AP Courses
The Right Academic Support for Creative Careers
Strategy: AP courses for arts students serve two purposes — keeping academic options open (in case you want a dual-degree program) and building skills that directly reinforce your creative work (art history, English, statistics).

🔴 Essential

AP Studio Art: 2D Design AP Studio Art: Drawing AP Art History AP English Language AP Music Theory
  • AP Studio Art (2D/Drawing/3D) — Directly builds your portfolio. A 5 submitted to colleges is a strong credential. The best AP for serious visual artists.
  • AP Art History — Context for all visual work. Understand what came before you. Required knowledge at every top art school.
  • AP English Language — Artists need to communicate their vision in writing. Client presentations, artist statements, grant applications all require strong writing.
  • AP Music Theory — For music-track students, this is foundational. Composition, harmony, sight reading.

🟡 Highly Recommended

AP CS Principles AP Statistics AP Psychology AP Micro/Macro Economics
  • AP CS Principles — Digital design, UX, game design, and motion graphics all require coding. Web/interactive designers who can't code are at a disadvantage.
  • AP Stats — UX research, advertising analytics, journalism. Data literacy is increasingly essential in every creative field.
  • AP Economics — Artists and designers who understand business earn significantly more. Freelance rates, contract negotiation, business structure.

🟢 Field-Specific

AP Calc (Architecture) AP Physics (Architecture) AP Gov (Journalism) AP World History (Film)

Architecture requires structural knowledge — AP Calc and Physics are strongly recommended. Journalism benefits from AP Government and History. Film benefits from broad cultural knowledge.

Extracurriculars
Build a Body of Work, Not a Resume
The rule for arts: Your portfolio IS your extracurricular. Competitions, exhibitions, performances, and publications are the validation. Don't join clubs to join clubs — make work, enter competitions, and find real audiences.

🏆 Tier 1 — By Field

  • Visual Art: Scholastic Art & Writing Awards (Gold Key = national recognition), local gallery exhibitions, juried shows
  • Film: Produce and submit short films to student film festivals (Sundance Ignite, Student Academy Awards, SXSW Student Competition)
  • Music: All-State Orchestra/Band/Choir. Original compositions performed publicly. Music production portfolio (SoundCloud/Spotify).
  • Design: Real client work (brand identity, websites, posters). Document on Behance or a personal site. Design competitions (Adobe Design Achievement Awards).
  • Architecture: AIA Architecture Competition for High Schoolers, DesignPhiladelphia competitions. Build physical models.

🥈 Tier 2

  • School newspaper — Photography editor, arts editor
  • Theater — Lead roles, stage/set design, director
  • Yearbook — Photo editor, design director
  • Art club — Only if you're leading and producing real work
  • Community mural or public art project — Creates permanent, visible portfolio work
  • YouTube/podcast/blog with consistent audience

🛠️ Skills to Build Before College

  • Adobe Creative Suite — Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, Premiere Pro, After Effects. Learn all of them. Free for students in many districts.
  • Figma — Free, industry-standard UI/UX design tool
  • Procreate — Industry standard for digital illustration (iPad)
  • DAW (music production) — Logic Pro, Ableton, GarageBand
  • 3D modeling — Blender (free), Cinema 4D, SketchUp

Creative Fields
Which Creative Career?
🖥️ UX / Product Design
$85K → $250K+ (FAANG)
Design digital products at tech companies. The highest-paying design career. Requires both visual design skills and systems thinking. Proficiency in Figma is the baseline. Portfolio of case studies is everything. Carnegie Mellon, RISD, and SCAD are top feeders.
🏗️ Architecture
$60K (entry) → $200K+ (principal)
Design buildings and spaces. Requires 5-year B.Arch or 4-yr BA + M.Arch. Followed by 3+ years of internship (AXP) and licensure exams (ARE). The path is long but the creative reward is extraordinary. SHoP, Zaha Hadid, BIG, and Bjarke Ingels are the elite firms.
🎨 Graphic / Brand Design
$45K (entry) → $180K+ (CDO)
Visual communication for brands, products, and organizations. In-house at companies (Apple, Nike, Google) or at design agencies (Pentagram, Wolff Olins). Typography, brand systems, layout. Adobe Illustrator + Figma are your tools.
🎬 Film & Television
$30K (PA) → millions (director)
Write, direct, produce, edit, or shoot. The most competitive creative industry. Most careers begin with years of low-paying assistant/PA work. Connections in LA and NYC dominate. Short films and YouTube shorts are the modern demo reel.
🎵 Music
Variable → $millions (top artists)
Performance, composition, production, music supervision, sync licensing, A&R, music business. Diversify income streams: streaming is low-pay, live performance + sync licensing + teaching build sustainable careers. Music tech (Spotify, Ableton) is growing fast.
📸 Photography
$32K (editorial) → $200K+ (commercial)
Commercial photography (advertising, product, architecture) pays 5x editorial (news, stock). Build toward commercial or specialize in one industry (food, fashion, real estate). Strong portfolio + licensing knowledge is the business foundation.
🎮 Game Design
$65K → $180K+ (senior)
Design the systems, levels, and experiences in games. AAA studios, indie, mobile, or VR. CS overlap is significant — the best game designers can code. Unity and Unreal Engine are the platforms. SCAD, NYU, CMU are the top schools for this track.
📰 Journalism & Communications
$38K → $150K+ (senior editor/anchor)
Print is declining; digital, video, newsletter, and podcast journalism is growing. NYT, Washington Post, and Substack are where the opportunities are. Communications and PR are more stable and pay better at senior levels than traditional journalism.
✏️ Animation & Motion Graphics
$52K → $160K+ (senior animator)
2D animation, 3D animation, VFX, motion graphics. Disney, Pixar, Industrial Light & Magic, Netflix Animation are the elite employers. Blender is free and industry-usable. CalArts is the pipeline school. A strong reel on YouTube/Vimeo gets you hired.
✍️ Creative Writing
$35K (publishing) → $millions (top authors)
Fiction, nonfiction, screenwriting, copywriting. Most creative writers combine writing with another career (teaching, tech writing, UX writing, content strategy). MFA programs from Iowa, NYU, Michigan are the elite. Content strategy and UX writing are growing, high-paying adjacent careers.
Resources
Tools, Communities & Competitions
🛠️ Essential Tools (Free/Student)
Free Design Tool
Figma
The industry standard UI/UX design tool. Free for students. Every product designer uses this. Learn it before college — it will set you apart immediately.
Student Discount
Adobe Creative Cloud
Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, Premiere Pro, After Effects, Lightroom. ~$35/mo for students. Master at least Photoshop + Illustrator before college.
Free 3D
Blender
Free, open-source 3D modeling and animation. Industry-quality. Used in Netflix productions. The best free tool for 3D artists, game designers, and architects.
Portfolio Platform
Behance
The global design portfolio platform (owned by Adobe). Every designer should have a Behance portfolio. It's how you get discovered by studios and recruiters.
Digital Drawing
Procreate (iPad)
The industry-standard digital illustration tool. $12.99 one-time purchase. Used by professional illustrators, concept artists, and animators worldwide. One purchase, no subscription.
Design
Canva (Pro, Free for Students)
Fast, accessible design for communications and presentations. Not a substitute for Adobe, but excellent for quick production and accessible to beginners.
🏆 Competitions & Programs
Competition
Scholastic Art & Writing Awards
The most prestigious national competition for HS artists and writers. A Gold Key is nationally recognized and highly respected by art schools. Categories include photography, design, film, writing, and fine art.
Competition
AIA Architecture Competition
American Institute of Architects high school design competition. Demonstrates architecture interest and shows you can think spatially and conceptually.
Film Program
Sundance Ignite
Sundance Institute's program for emerging filmmakers aged 18–25. Submit a short film. A selection or fellowship is an extraordinary credential for film school applications.
Competition
Student Academy Awards
Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences (the Oscar people) run this competition for student filmmakers. Division I is for HS students. A finalist here is exceptional.
📚 Books & Learning
YouTube
The Art Assignment
PBS series on contemporary art, art history, and creative practice. Intelligent, well-produced, and genuinely useful for any artist building context for their work.
Book
Steal Like an Artist — Austin Kleon
The most honest and useful guide to building a creative practice. Short, visual, actionable. Read this the summer before starting to build your portfolio seriously.
Book
The War of Art — Steven Pressfield
On overcoming resistance, doing creative work, and treating it as a profession. The essential mindset book for anyone who wants to make art professionally. Read once per year.
Book
How Buildings Learn — Stewart Brand
How architecture changes over time and why. The best introduction to thinking about buildings intelligently. Essential for aspiring architects — and genuinely interesting for everyone.
Music Production
Produce Like A Pro (YouTube)
Learn music production, mixing, and mastering. One of the best free channels for aspiring music producers and audio engineers. Covers every DAW.
Courses
Skillshare
Short-form design, illustration, photography, and creative courses taught by working professionals. Free trial + student discounts. Excellent for specific skill gaps.
💡 Career Reality Checks
The portfolio rule: No amount of GPA, school prestige, or club membership compensates for a weak portfolio in the arts. Every hour you spend on real work is worth ten hours in any classroom. Start building now and never stop.
The business rule: Artists who understand contracts, pricing, licensing, copyright, and business development earn 3–5x those who don't. Take a business or entrepreneurship course in high school. Read your contracts. Understand how creative rights work before you give them away.
