🏢 Business

"The art and science of building organizations that last."
What is Business?
The Broadest Degree.

Business is the most versatile degree available — and the most popular. Every organization in every industry needs people who understand strategy, operations, marketing, and finance. The tradeoff is that a generic "Business" degree from an average school is worth less than a specialized degree (engineering, CS, finance) from any school.

The key is to specialize. Business + Consulting track, Business + Entrepreneurship, Business + Tech, Business + Finance — these combinations are powerful. Pure generalist business is less differentiated.

The MBA question: Most elite business careers (McKinsey, Bain, BCG; Goldman Sachs; PE; C-suite) now prefer an undergraduate specialized degree followed by an MBA from HBS/Wharton/Booth. Plan for the MBA as your true launchpad for the elite track.

High School Roadmap

  • 9th: Start a micro-business (lawn care, reselling, tutoring). Any real entrepreneurial experience is gold.
  • 10th: DECA, FBLA. AP Econ. Learn Excel fundamentals. Read business news (WSJ, Bloomberg).
  • 11th: AP Calc, AP Stats, AP Macro. Compete in national DECA/FBLA. Intern at any business, firm, or startup.
  • 12th: Apply to top undergraduate business programs (Wharton, Ross, Stern, Goizueta, etc.) or plan a strong major to supplement a liberal arts degree.

Extracurriculars
Show Business Instinct

🏆 Tier 1

  • DECA (national level) — Business competitions in marketing, finance, management. National DECA is highly respected by top business schools.
  • Start a real business — Even a small one. Lemonade stand economics don't count. But a tutoring service, Etsy shop, or app with real customers does.
  • Business internship — Any company. Marketing, operations, sales, admin. Real exposure counts.
  • Pitch competition winner — Local or national business plan competitions. Diamond Challenge, NFTE, etc.

🥈 Tier 2

  • FBLA (Future Business Leaders of America)
  • School store management or club treasurer
  • Investment / Finance Club (founded or led)
  • Junior Achievement company program
  • Social enterprise or fundraising initiative with measurable impact

Business Tracks
Which Business Career?
📊 Management Consulting
$90K → $500K+ (Partner)
Help companies solve strategic problems. MBB (McKinsey, Bain, BCG) is the elite tier. Requires top GPA, case interview preparation, and typically a top-10 undergrad or MBA. Excellent exit opportunities.
📣 Marketing / Brand Management
$55K → $200K+ (CMO)
Build and manage brands. P&G, Unilever, Nike, Google. Brand management is the classic consumer goods track. Digital marketing (SEO, SEM, analytics) is the fastest-growing segment.
🚀 Entrepreneurship / Startups
$0 → unlimited
The highest-risk, highest-reward track. Build something from scratch. No school can teach you this — you learn by doing. Stanford and MIT environments are unparalleled for startup culture.
📦 Supply Chain / Operations
$62K → $200K (VP Ops)
Amazon, Tesla, Apple, Walmart. The backbone of every product company. Data-driven, increasingly technical. Industrial Engineering overlap is significant. Huge demand and growing pay.
🌍 International Business
$60K → $200K+
Global markets, trade, international strategy. Multilingual fluency is a major differentiator. Multinational corporations, NGOs, the State Department. Georgetown and Thunderbird are strong.
👔 Human Resources / Talent
$55K → $250K (CHRO)
Talent acquisition, organizational design, culture. Often underestimated but CHROs at Fortune 500 companies earn significant compensation. HR analytics is a growing, high-value specialization.
Resources
Business Self-Education
News
Wall Street Journal
Read this daily. Student subscriptions are discounted. Understanding current business news is expected in interviews at every level.
Strategy
Harvard Business Review
Articles on management, strategy, leadership, and organizational behavior. Free articles available. Read 2–3 articles per week. This is what MBAs read.
Competition
DECA
The premier business competition for high school students. Compete in marketing, finance, hospitality, management. National-level achievement is meaningful for admissions.
Course
Yale Financial Markets (Coursera)
Robert Shiller's legendary course, free to audit. One of the best introductions to how financial markets work. Mention this in your application essays.
📚 Essential Reading
Book
Zero to One — Peter Thiel
Stanford's CS183 course notes. How to build companies that create new things. Required reading for anyone interested in entrepreneurship or venture capital.
Book
The Lean Startup — Eric Ries
The methodology that changed how startups are built. MVP, iterate fast, measure what matters. The operating manual for modern entrepreneurship.
Book
Good to Great — Jim Collins
Research-based analysis of what separates great companies from good ones. The most cited business book in MBA programs. Foundational strategic thinking.
