📈 Finance

"Where math, markets, and ambition meet."
What is Finance?
Money, Markets, and Power.

Finance is the study of how money flows through the world: how it's raised, invested, allocated, and multiplied. Finance graduates can enter some of the highest-paying careers available to undergraduate students — investment banking, private equity, hedge funds, and quantitative trading regularly offer total compensation packages exceeding $200K–$500K+ within 5–10 years of graduation.

Finance is unique because the path matters enormously. Investment banking at Goldman Sachs → private equity → MBA from Harvard Business School is a well-defined track. Understanding this pipeline early is a massive advantage.

Key distinction: Finance (the major) is different from Accounting, Economics, and Business. Finance focuses on valuation, capital markets, and investment decisions. Accounting is backward-looking (recording transactions). Economics is theoretical. Finance is applied and highly quantitative at the elite level.

High School Roadmap for Finance

  • 9th: Open a paper trading account (Investopedia). Start reading the WSJ or Bloomberg. Take the most rigorous math available.
  • 10th: AP Economics. Start a stock market or investment club. Learn Excel fundamentals (VLOOKUP, pivot tables, basic models).
  • 11th: AP Calc BC, AP Statistics. Compete in investing competitions. Try building a simple DCF model on YouTube tutorials. Shadow a professional if possible.
  • 12th: AP Macro, AP Micro. Prep your story around markets and finance. Apply to target/semi-target schools with strong alumni networks on Wall Street.

What Finance Looks Like Day-to-Day

  • Investment banking: Build financial models, pitch decks, 80-hour weeks
  • Asset management: Research companies, build theses, manage portfolios
  • Private equity: Analyze buyout targets, oversee portfolio companies
  • Quant: Write algorithms, backtest strategies, manage risk models
AP Courses
Build the Quantitative Foundation

🔴 Essential

AP Calculus BC AP Statistics AP Microeconomics AP Macroeconomics
  • AP Calc BC — Quantitative finance requires calculus. This is your baseline. A 5 is expected at target schools.
  • AP Statistics — Risk, probability, and data analysis are core finance skills. Strong signal for quant finance.
  • AP Micro + Macro — Understanding how markets and economies function is foundational. Take both.

🟡 Highly Recommended

AP CS A AP US History AP English Lang AP Psychology
  • AP CS A — Even non-quant finance roles now require coding. Excel → Python → SQL is the progression.
  • AP History + English — Finance involves extensive writing and presentation. Communication skills are how you get promoted.
  • AP Psychology — Behavioral finance is a real field. Understanding human decision-making is increasingly valued.
Extracurriculars
Demonstrate Financial Sophistication Early

🏆 Tier 1

  • Investment / Finance Club — President — Lead a club that manages a mock portfolio, presents investment pitches, hosts speakers. This is the #1 signal.
  • Stock pitch competition — CNBC's Invest in You, CFA Institute Research Challenge. Win or place competitively.
  • Internship at a financial firm — Even unpaid, even at a small local firm. Any real exposure is extraordinary for a high schooler.
  • Personal investment portfolio — Open a real account (with parental help). Document your thesis and returns. Talk about this in essays.

🥈 Tier 2

  • Build a financial model (DCF, LBO intro) — document and share it
  • Business/entrepreneurship competitions (DECA, FBLA)
  • Write a finance blog or newsletter with real stock analysis
  • Shadow a banker, financial advisor, or analyst
  • AMC/AIME math competition — quantitative finance doors open with strong math credentials

🗣️ Soft Skills That Matter in Finance

  • Public speaking and persuasive presentation
  • Professional writing and email etiquette
  • Networking — the finance world runs on relationships
  • Excel proficiency (non-negotiable in any finance role)

Finance Tracks
Which Finance Career?
🏦 Investment Banking
$170K → $1M+ (MD)
Advise companies on mergers, acquisitions, and capital raises. Incredibly demanding hours (80–100/week as analyst). But the pay and exit opportunities (PE, hedge fund, corporate, MBA) are elite. The classic "break-in" career.
🏢 Private Equity
$200K → $5M+ (Partner)
Buy companies, improve them, sell them at profit. 2-and-20 fee structure generates massive carried interest. Typically requires 2–3 years IB first. The most competitive exit from investment banking. Long-term wealth accumulation is exceptional.
📉 Hedge Funds
$150K → $unlimited (PM)
Trade securities using sophisticated strategies: long/short equity, macro, event-driven. Star portfolio managers can earn tens of millions. Extreme performance pressure. Ken Griffin (Citadel), Steve Cohen (Point72) are examples of the ceiling.
📐 Quantitative Finance
$150K → $2M+ (senior)
Apply math and CS to trading. Jane Street, Citadel, Two Sigma, DE Shaw. Require exceptional math — think AIME/Putnam level. The CS–finance overlap pays the highest in the industry. Start with competitive math in high school.
📊 Asset Management / AM
$90K → $400K+ (PM)
Manage portfolios for institutions or individuals. BlackRock, Vanguard, Fidelity, T. Rowe Price. More lifestyle-friendly than IB/PE but still highly selective at top firms. CFA designation is important in this track.
🏦 Corporate Finance / FP&A
$65K → $200K (CFO path)
Work inside a company managing its finances. Budgeting, forecasting, capital allocation. Better hours than banking. CFO track for ambitious candidates. Less glamorous but highly stable and skills transfer across industries.
🏘️ Real Estate Finance
$70K → $300K+ (principal)
REITs, real estate private equity, commercial real estate lending. Less competitive to break into than traditional PE. Strong income potential. Chicago, NYC, LA are hubs. Often overlaps with development and construction.
🌱 Venture Capital
$80K → $5M+ (GP)
Fund early-stage startups. Requires either IB/PE background or successful entrepreneurship. Extremely difficult to break into without a track record. The carry on a successful fund is transformative wealth.

Finance Self-Education Materials
Community
Wall Street Oasis
The #1 finance career forum. Salary data, interview prep, breaking-in guides, and a community of professionals. Essential resource.
Guide
Mergers & Inquisitions
The most comprehensive free resource for breaking into investment banking. Covers everything from networking to modeling to interview prep.
News
Wall Street Journal
Read this daily. Students often get discounted access. Understanding current markets and deals is essential for interviews and general literacy.
Courses
Corporate Finance Institute (CFI)
Free and paid courses in financial modeling, valuation, Excel, and accounting. Highly recognized certifications. Start with Excel Fundamentals.
Reference
Investopedia
The encyclopedia of finance. Look up any term here. Also has a free stock market simulator to practice investing without real money.
Tool
Yahoo Finance
Free financial data on any public company. Read 10-K filings, check earnings, track your mock portfolio. This is your starting research tool.
📚 Essential Reading
Book
The Intelligent Investor — Benjamin Graham
Warren Buffett's bible. Foundational value investing philosophy. Read this before any stock pitch or interview. Chapters 8 and 20 are essential.
Book
Liar's Poker — Michael Lewis
The definitive insider account of Wall Street culture at Salomon Brothers. Essential cultural context for understanding investment banking.
Book
The Big Short — Michael Lewis
How a handful of investors bet against the housing market. The best narrative explanation of complex financial instruments (CDOs, CDS).
Book
Investment Banking (Rosenbaum & Pearl)
The actual IB technical interview bible. Covers valuation methodologies (DCF, comps, precedent transactions). Read before interviews in senior year.
