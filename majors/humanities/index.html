📚 Humanities

"The study of what it means to be human — and how to influence it."
What are the Humanities?
Think. Write. Lead.

The humanities encompass English, History, Political Science, Philosophy, Sociology, Psychology, Linguistics, Religious Studies, and related fields. These disciplines train your ability to think critically, write persuasively, analyze complex systems, and understand human behavior at scale.

Humanities degrees are often misunderstood. The career paths are real — but they often require pairing your degree with a clear professional skill (law, consulting, communications, policy) or graduate school (JD, MBA, PhD). The smartest humanities majors treat their degree as a foundation, not a destination.

The secret: The most successful people in media, politics, law, and tech leadership often have humanities degrees. Barack Obama (political science), Elon Musk (economics + physics), Steve Jobs (Reed, dropped out but studied calligraphy). Critical thinking and communication are the most durable career skills.

High School Roadmap

  • 9th: Read voraciously — fiction, nonfiction, history. Take Honors English. Start a journal. Develop an opinion.
  • 10th: AP World History or AP European History. AP English Language. Start debating and writing for publication.
  • 11th: AP US History, AP Language. Compete in debate, Model UN, journalism. Develop a clear intellectual interest area.
  • 12th: AP Literature, AP Government, AP Psychology. Apply to schools with strong programs in your chosen field. Your essays matter more in humanities than STEM.

Career Paths for Humanities Majors

  • Law (most JD holders have humanities degrees)
  • Government and Public Policy
  • Journalism and Media
  • Consulting (McKinsey, Deloitte hire English and history majors)
  • Nonprofit and NGO leadership
  • Education and academia
  • Marketing and communications
  • Tech (product management, UX research, content strategy)

Humanities Fields
Which Humanities Discipline?
📖 English / Literature
$40K → $120K (senior comms)
Reading, analysis, writing. Prepares for law, publishing, education, media, communications, and content strategy. The writing skills you build have value in every industry.
🏛️ Political Science
$48K → $250K+ (law/policy)
Government, international relations, political theory. Gateway to law school, government service, NGOs, think tanks, journalism, and elected office. The most career-flexible humanities major.
📜 History
$40K → $200K+ (law/academia)
Research, analysis, and synthesis of the past. Exceptional writing and argumentation skills. Most history majors go to law school, graduate school, or education. The second-highest average LSAT score after philosophy.
🧠 Psychology
$42K → $200K+ (PhD/clinical)
Human behavior, cognition, emotion, mental health. BS → clinical PhD or PsyD for practice. Also valuable for UX research, HR, marketing, and organizational behavior. Growing rapidly.
📐 Philosophy
$42K → $300K+ (law/tech ethics)
Logic, ethics, epistemology. The highest average LSAT score of any major. Valued in law, policy, AI ethics, and tech leadership. Teaches systematic thinking that's applicable everywhere.
🌐 Sociology / Anthropology
$38K → $100K (research/policy)
Social systems, cultures, inequality, organizations. Valuable for policy work, public health, social services, academic research, and UX/market research. Graduate school is often the path to advancement.
Resources
Essential Reading & Tools
Reading
The New Yorker
The gold standard of long-form journalism and literary culture. Read at least one article per week. This is what excellent writing looks like.
Opinion / Argument
NYT Opinion
Model argumentation and persuasive writing. Read columns you disagree with. Understanding the strongest version of opposing views is essential for debate and writing.
Competition
National History Day
The most prestigious history competition for HS students. Research, document, and present historical analysis. Excellent for college applications.
Free Books
Project Gutenberg
Free access to every book whose copyright has expired. All of Shakespeare, Plato, Dickens, Austen, Hemingway. Read widely — the breadth shows.
