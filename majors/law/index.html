⚖️ Pre-Law

"Argue better. Think sharper. Win more."
Pre-law is not a major. You can study anything in undergrad and apply to law school. But some majors give you a significant advantage on the LSAT and in law school itself. See the APs & Focus tab for the best choices.
The Law School Path
3 Years After Undergrad.

Law is a 3-year graduate program (JD) after a 4-year undergraduate degree. Unlike medicine, you apply to law school in your senior year of college and begin at 22. The path is: undergrad → LSAT → law school → bar exam → practice. The top programs (Harvard, Yale, Columbia, Stanford, Chicago, NYU, Penn — the "T14") are where elite legal careers are built.

Two things matter above all else for law school admission: GPA and LSAT score. A 3.9 GPA and 175 LSAT will get you into every school. The LSAT is a test of logic, reading, and argument analysis — skills you can build starting in high school.

High School → Law School Roadmap

  • 9th: Take the hardest English and history classes. Start debating. Read books about law (To Kill a Mockingbird, Just Mercy).
  • 10th: Join Debate team. Mock Trial if available. AP Government and Politics. AP English Language.
  • 11th: AP Lang, AP US History, AP Gov. Compete in debate at invitational/state level. Research internship or court observation.
  • 12th: AP Literature, AP Psychology, AP Stats. Apply to colleges with strong pre-law advising and debate programs. Political science or philosophy are solid major choices.

Best Undergrad Majors for Law School

  • Political Science — The most common pre-law major. Covers constitutional law, political theory, policy.
  • Philosophy — Logic and ethics training. Highest average LSAT scores of any major. Argumentation is law.
  • English — Close reading and persuasive writing. Essential skills for legal practice.
  • History — Legal reasoning is historical reasoning. Research and writing overlap perfectly.
  • Economics — Excellent for corporate law, antitrust, or law and economics tracks at T14 schools.
  • CS or Engineering — Patent law, technology law. The MOST differentiated background — rare and valuable.

What Law School Admission Wants

  • GPA 3.9+ (for Yale/Harvard), 3.7+ (for T14 generally)
  • LSAT 173+ (Yale), 170+ (Harvard/Stanford), 168+ (other T14)
  • Compelling personal statement with clear narrative
  • Leadership and community impact (not just clubs)
  • Diversity statement if applicable
  • Strong recommendation letters from professors who know you
AP Courses
Build Argument, Reasoning, and Research Skills

🔴 Essential

AP English Language AP English Literature AP US History AP Government & Politics AP Psychology
  • AP English Lang — Argumentation, rhetorical analysis, evidence. The LSAT is 50% this. Take it seriously.
  • AP US History — Legal reasoning is embedded in American history. Constitutional history, civil rights, legislation.
  • AP Gov — Supreme Court cases, constitutional law, federal system. Direct LSAT and law school relevance.
  • AP Psych — Criminal law, evidence, and trial practice all involve psychology. Behavioral science on the LSAT.

🟡 Highly Recommended

AP Statistics AP Economics AP World History AP Philosophy (if available)
  • AP Stats — Expert witness testimony, forensic science, and data-heavy cases require statistical literacy.
  • AP Econ — Corporate law, antitrust, and regulatory law are heavily economic. Law and Economics is a major field.
  • Logic courses — Any course in formal logic or philosophy is extremely LSAT-relevant. Logical Reasoning is 50% of the exam.
Extracurriculars
Argue, Lead, and Demonstrate Impact

🏆 Tier 1

  • Policy Debate (CX) — The single most impactful EC for law school. Builds argument structure, research skills, and public speaking simultaneously. Nationally competitive debaters are prioritized.
  • Mock Trial — Captain — Direct legal skills practice. Present cases, cross-examine witnesses. Regional and national competition demonstrates commitment.
  • Congressional Debate / Model Congress — Legislative process, bill writing, parliamentary procedure.
  • Internship at a law firm or courthouse — Observe depositions, hearings, client meetings. Even administrative work counts.

🥈 Tier 2

  • Model United Nations (MUN) — diplomatic negotiation and research skills
  • Student government — Legislative process, leadership, constituency representation
  • School newspaper — Editor-in-Chief or investigative journalist
  • Community justice or advocacy volunteering
  • Write about a legal case or issue — publish in your school paper or a journal

Programs

  • Summer Law Institute programs — Georgetown, Penn, and many T14s offer HS summer programs
  • YMCA Youth & Government — Mock state legislature. Statewide program with real impact.
  • Close Up Washington — Civic education program, government immersion

Legal Specialties
What Kind of Lawyer?
🏢 Corporate / M&A Law
$225K → $3M+ (partner)
Advise companies on mergers, acquisitions, deals, and governance. The dominant BigLaw practice area. Requires attention to detail, deal stamina, and business sophistication. Cravath, Sullivan & Cromwell, Skadden are the elite firms.
💡 Intellectual Property / Patent
$190K → $600K+ (partner)
Protect inventions, trademarks, and copyrights. Patent lawyers with engineering/CS degrees are the most valuable attorneys in Silicon Valley. USPTO registration requires a technical degree — plan accordingly.
⚖️ Litigation / Trial Law
$70K (public) → $2M+ (BigLaw)
Argue cases in court. Can be criminal or civil. The most visible and dramatic legal career. Top litigators are celebrities in legal circles. Requires exceptional oral and written advocacy skills.
🌍 International Law
$80K → $400K+
Trade agreements, human rights, international arbitration, foreign investment. NYU, Georgetown, and Columbia are the top feeders. Learning a second language is a significant career advantage.
🏛️ Public Interest / Government
$55K → $200K (senior)
Work for the government, nonprofits, or advocacy organizations. ACLU, DOJ, public defenders, elected officials. Lower pay but loan forgiveness (PSLF) programs and extraordinary impact. Yale and Georgetown are the top schools for this path.
💻 Technology & Privacy Law
$200K → $1M+
The fastest-growing legal specialty. AI regulation, data privacy (GDPR, CCPA), antitrust in tech, cybersecurity. A CS background + JD is an extraordinary combination with virtually unlimited demand.

📚 Must-Read Books
Book
Just Mercy — Bryan Stevenson
A civil rights attorney's account of representing death row inmates. The most powerful argument for public interest law. Bryan Stevenson is Harvard Law. Read this before your personal statement.
Book
The Bramble Bush — Karl Llewellyn
The classic introduction to what law school is and what it teaches. Written by one of America's greatest legal scholars. Short and essential.
Book
One L — Scott Turow
Diary of a Harvard Law 1L. Honest, gripping account of what the first year of law school is actually like. Required reading for anyone seriously considering law.
