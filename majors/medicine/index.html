🩺 Pre-Medicine

"The longest road, with the most meaningful destination."
The timeline: 4 years undergrad → 4 years medical school → 3–7 years residency → optional fellowship (1–3 yrs). You will be practicing medicine roughly 10–15 years after starting undergrad. Plan accordingly.
The Pre-Med Path
A Decade-Long Journey.

Pre-medicine is not a major — it is a track. You can major in anything and apply to medical school as long as you complete the required prerequisites (bio, chem, ochem, physics, math, psych). Most pre-med students major in Biology or Chemistry, but Neuroscience, Biochemistry, and even English or Philosophy are perfectly valid choices.

What matters for medical school admission: GPA (3.7+ is competitive), MCAT score (515+ for top schools), clinical experience, research, and your personal statement. Starting high school with a clear plan gives you years of lead time to build each of these elements.

High School → Med School Roadmap

  • 9th: AP Bio, Health & Wellness. Volunteer at a hospital or clinic — even administrative work counts. Start building your narrative.
  • 10th: AP Chemistry. Shadow a doctor for at least 40 hours. Join a health/science club or start one.
  • 11th: AP Bio, AP Chem, AP Physics 1. Begin research if possible (email professors). This is your hardest AP year — show you can handle the load.
  • 12th: AP Physics 2 or AP Biology 2 if available. AP Psychology. Apply to colleges with strong pre-med support and research opportunities.

What Medical Schools Actually Want

  • GPA 3.7+ — Science GPA matters most. Don't get a B in Orgo.
  • MCAT 515+ — For top 20 schools. Median MCAT at Harvard Med: 522.
  • Clinical hours 200+ — Actual patient contact. Volunteering in a hospital, scribing, EMT work.
  • Research 500+ hours — Even better: a publication. Basic science or clinical research both count.
  • Shadowing 100+ hours — See what a doctor actually does in multiple specialties.
  • Leadership — Lead something meaningful. Founding a health initiative counts more than joining one.
  • Personal statement — Tell a clear story about why medicine specifically. "I want to help people" is not enough.

Pre-Med Prerequisites (Must Complete in Undergrad)

  • Biology I & II with lab
  • General Chemistry I & II with lab
  • Organic Chemistry I & II with lab
  • Physics I & II with lab
  • Biochemistry (most schools now require)
  • Statistics / Math
  • English / Writing
  • Psychology and Sociology (MCAT-tested)
AP Courses
Build the Science Foundation

🔴 Essential

AP Biology AP Chemistry AP Physics 1 & 2 AP Psychology AP Statistics
  • AP Bio — Directly mirrors MCAT biology content. A 5 here is foundational.
  • AP Chem — General chemistry is a pre-med prereq. Build mastery here, not in college.
  • AP Physics 1 & 2 — The MCAT tests physics. Kinematics, fluid dynamics, circuits, optics.
  • AP Psych — Behavioral and Social Sciences is 25% of the MCAT. AP Psych gives you a huge head start.
  • AP Stats — Research design and data interpretation are tested on the MCAT and expected in research settings.

🟡 Recommended

AP Calc AB/BC AP English Lang/Lit AP US History
  • AP Calc — Required for many universities' physics sequences. Also appears lightly on MCAT (logarithms, exponential decay).
  • AP English — CARS (Critical Analysis and Reasoning Skills) is 30% of the MCAT. It tests reading comprehension of humanities passages. AP Lit/Lang builds this skill directly.
MCAT note: The MCAT tests Biology, Biochemistry, Chemistry, Physics, Psychology, Sociology, and Critical Reading. The more AP courses you take in these areas, the less content you'll need to relearn when studying for the MCAT in junior year of college.
Extracurriculars
Clinical + Research + Leadership

🏆 Tier 1

  • Hospital volunteering — Patient-facing. Emergency department, cancer ward, pediatrics. Minimum 100 hours before med school apps. Start in 9th grade.
  • Research internship — University lab, hospital research department, NIH summer program. Even better: a poster or publication.
  • EMT certification — Gold standard clinical experience. You are actually treating patients. More impactful than most volunteering.
  • Disease awareness / health initiative — Founding something (a diabetes awareness club, a health fair for underserved communities) shows leadership AND mission alignment.

🥈 Tier 2

  • Doctor shadowing (multiple specialties, 100+ hrs total)
  • Medical scribe (paid, extremely high-value clinical experience)
  • Health-related science clubs (BioClub, Pre-Med Society)
  • Tutoring fellow students in science (teaching = mastery)
  • Community health outreach programs

Programs to Apply To

  • NIH Summer Internship Program — Free, prestigious, nationally recognized
  • Research Science Institute (RSI) — MIT-based 6-week summer research program for HS juniors. Very competitive.
  • PRIMES (MIT) — Math/CS research for HS students
  • Local hospital volunteer programs — apply freshman year

Medical Specialties
Which Doctor Do You Want to Be?
🧠 Neurology / Neurosurgery
$350K–$1M+
Diagnose and treat brain and nervous system disorders. Neurosurgery is the most demanding and highest-paying surgical specialty. Neurology (non-surgical) is intellectually fascinating. Longest training in medicine.
❤️ Cardiology
$400K–$700K
Heart disease is America's #1 killer. Cardiologists are in extraordinary demand. Interventional cardiology (placing stents, performing procedures) pays at the top of the specialty. Requires IM residency + 3-yr fellowship.
🔬 Oncology
$350K–$600K
Diagnose and treat cancer. Medical, surgical, and radiation oncology are distinct tracks. High emotional toll but among the most meaningful work in medicine. Growing demand as cancer incidence rises.
🧬 Genetics / Genomics
$220K–$380K
The frontier of medicine. CRISPR, gene therapy, inherited diseases. Academic medicine and biotech overlap. Requires MD or MD/PhD. The future of precision medicine is here.
🩺 Primary Care / Family Medicine
$185K–$250K
The backbone of the healthcare system. Lower pay than specialties but highest patient relationships, best hours, and most flexible practice settings. Physician shortage is severe — demand is surging.
👶 Pediatrics
$185K–$280K
Care for children from birth through adolescence. Subspecialties (pediatric cardiology, oncology) pay significantly more. The most joyful practice setting for many physicians.
Resources
Pre-Med Toolkit
Free Prep
Khan Academy MCAT
Comprehensive MCAT content review, completely free. Start familiarizing yourself with MCAT content in 11th grade. Use this alongside AP Bio and Chem.
Official
AAMC (Official)
The organization that runs the MCAT and medical school applications (AMCAS). The most authoritative source on what medical schools want.
Community
r/premed
Active community of 400K+ pre-med students. Real GPA/MCAT stats, cycle data, advice. Invaluable for understanding what med school applications actually look like.
Tool
Shadowing.me
Platform that connects pre-med students with physicians for shadowing opportunities. Finding shadowing is the biggest challenge — use this.
Research
PubMed Central
Free access to millions of medical research papers. Learn to read scientific literature. This skill is required in med school and valued in applications.
Internship
ACEs Intern (NIH)
NIH-affiliated research opportunities for high school students. Government-funded science research. One of the most prestigious HS research programs available.
📚 Books for Future Physicians
Book
When Breath Becomes Air — Paul Kalanithi
A neurosurgeon confronts his own terminal cancer. The most beautiful and honest account of why medicine matters. Read this before writing your personal statement.
Book
The House of God — Samuel Shem
The brutally honest novel about medical internship. Controversial and important. Gives you a realistic picture of what residency is actually like.
Book
Being Mortal — Atul Gawande
A surgeon's reflection on death, medicine, and what patients actually want at the end of life. Changes how you think about the purpose of medicine.
