🔬 Natural Sciences

"The pursuit of truth about how the universe actually works."
What are the Natural Sciences?
Understand Everything.

Natural science majors — Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Biochemistry, Neuroscience, Environmental Science, Astronomy — pursue fundamental understanding of how the physical and biological world works. Science degrees are the gateway to research careers, graduate school, medicine, biotechnology, policy, and more.

The honest picture: A pure science BS degree without a clear next step (PhD program, med school, industry role) can result in lower starting salaries than engineering or business. The key is having a plan: academia → PhD → professor/researcher; industry → biotech/pharma/government; medicine → med school.

High School Roadmap

  • 9th: Take Honors Biology and Chemistry. Start observing the world with scientific curiosity. Nature journals, science documentaries.
  • 10th: AP Biology or AP Chemistry. Science Olympiad. Try to shadow a researcher or lab technician.
  • 11th: AP Chemistry + AP Physics. Apply for summer research programs (RSI, PRIMES, HHMI). Science fair if your school has one.
  • 12th: AP Bio, AP Physics C. Intel ISEF if you have a project. Apply to universities with strong research departments in your specific interest.

Career Pathways from Science Degrees

  • Medicine (MD) — Biology, Biochemistry, or Chemistry are the classic pre-med paths
  • Research (PhD) — Graduate school → postdoc → professor or industry researcher
  • Biotech / Pharma — Drug development, clinical research, regulatory affairs
  • Government / Policy — FDA, EPA, NIH, Congressional science staff
  • Science Communication — Writing, journalism, museum work, education
  • Patent Law — Science degree + JD = patent attorney (very high pay)

What Research Actually Looks Like

  • Long hours in the lab with slow, incremental progress
  • Failure is normal — most experiments don't work the first time
  • Reading constantly: scientific papers, reviews, textbooks
  • Writing: grants, papers, presentations, lab reports
  • PhD programs typically take 4–6 years and are funded (you get paid, not tuition)

Extracurriculars
Research and Discovery

🏆 Tier 1

  • Intel/Regeneron ISEF or STS — The pinnacle of high school science. A finalist placement is more impressive to MIT, Caltech, and top research universities than almost anything else.
  • University research internship — Email professors directly. Persistence works. Even a semester of lab work is extraordinary.
  • Science Olympiad (nationals/state) — Breadth of scientific knowledge. Build, test, and experiment across 23 events.
  • RSI (Research Science Institute) — MIT-based. 6 weeks of real research mentored by faculty. The most competitive HS science program in the country.

🥈 Tier 2

  • Science club (president, with real projects)
  • Science fair (regional, state level)
  • PRIMES (MIT math/CS research for HS students)
  • Hospital volunteering (for pre-med science students)
  • Citizen science projects (NASA, iNaturalist, Zooniverse)

🏫 Summer Programs

  • NIH Summer Internship Program (competitive, federal pay)
  • Jackson Lab Summer Student Program (genetics)
  • HHMI Summer Research for HS students
  • Clark Scholars (TTU — 8-week paid research)
  • NASA High School Aerospace Scholars

Science Fields
Which Science?
🧬 Biology / Molecular Biology
$42K (BS) → $130K (PhD/industry)
The study of life at every level. Genetics, cell biology, evolution, ecology. Gateway to medicine, biotech, environmental science. CRISPR, gene editing, and synthetic biology are the frontier.
⚗️ Chemistry
$50K (BS) → $140K (PhD/industry)
Matter and its interactions. Organic, inorganic, physical, analytical, computational chemistry. Pharma, materials science, chemical engineering, and research all need chemists. Extremely versatile.
⚛️ Physics
$65K (BS) → $200K+ (finance/tech)
The most fundamental science. Mechanics, quantum, relativity, thermodynamics. Physics majors are prized in quantitative finance, tech, and engineering research. The most math-intensive science degree.
🧠 Neuroscience
$45K (BS) → $150K (PhD)
The brain and nervous system. One of the fastest-growing fields in science. Overlaps with psychology, medicine, AI, and engineering. The mysteries of consciousness make this field irresistible for many.
🌍 Environmental Science
$45K → $100K (senior)
Climate, ecology, conservation, water systems. Enormous public importance. Government, nonprofit, and energy industry employment. Growing rapidly as climate policy accelerates globally.
🔭 Astronomy / Astrophysics
$55K (BS) → $120K (PhD/postdoc)
The universe at the largest scales. Heavily mathematical and computational. Most careers require a PhD. Academic and government employment (NASA, national labs). One of the most intellectually thrilling fields.
Resources
Science Self-Education
Foundation
Khan Academy Science
Free biology, chemistry, physics courses with video instruction. Use to supplement AP courses and fill gaps. Essential free resource.
YouTube
Kurzgesagt
Beautiful animated science videos covering everything from cells to the universe. Builds intuition and excitement for science at every level.
News
Science News
Accessible science journalism covering recent breakthroughs. Read weekly to stay current on what's happening across all fields of science.
Competition
Regeneron ISEF
International Science and Engineering Fair. The most prestigious HS science competition. Start a research project in 9th or 10th grade and develop it over years.
Free Courses
MIT OCW Science
Free MIT courses in chemistry, physics, biology, and more. Take 5.111 (Principles of Chemical Science) to get ahead for freshman chemistry.
Research
PubMed
Access millions of scientific papers for free. Learn to read scientific literature in high school. This is the primary skill of science research.
